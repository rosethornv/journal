C.S. Lewis explores four distinct types of love in his book The Four Loves, each based on different aspects of human relationships. Here they are:

1. Storge (Affection)

  • Description: This is the love found within families, especially the natural bond between parent and child or between siblings.
  • Traits: Familiar, comfortable, often taken for granted, deeply rooted in shared life and experiences.
  • Example: A mother caring for her child or the affection between old friends.

2. Philia (Friendship)

  • Description: The love between friends, based on shared interests, values, or pursuits.
  • Traits: Rare, chosen, built on mutual respect and admiration, can be deep and enduring.
  • Example: Two people who connect through a shared passion, like music, literature, or a cause.

3. Eros (Romantic Love)

  • Description: Romantic, passionate love ‚Äì what we often think of as being ‚Äúin love.‚Äù
  • Traits: Intense, emotional, longing, not just sexual but includes a desire for union.
  • Example: Lovers who are captivated by each other and want to be wholly united.

4. Agape (Charity / Divine Love)

  • Description: Unconditional, selfless love ‚Äì the love of God for humanity and the love we are called to have for others.
  • Traits: Sacrificial, generous, not based on merit or emotion, but on will and commitment.
  • Example: Loving your neighbor or enemy, giving without expecting anything in return.

Lewis discusses how each love has strengths and potential pitfalls, especially when it's idolized or detached from divine love (Agape). He sees Agape as the highest form of love, the one that completes and purifies the others.
